📦 Physical Copies

Physical copies are available for select titles through DriveThruRPG's print-on-demand service. Check each game's page for availability. We're working on expanding our physical catalog — sign up for our newsletter to be notified when new print options launch.
Physical print-on-demand orders through DriveThruRPG ship internationally. Shipping rates and times vary by location and are calculated at checkout on DriveThruRPG.
For digital purchases, all sales are final as the product is delivered immediately upon purchase. For physical orders fulfilled through DriveThruRPG, please refer to DriveThruRPG's return policy. If you received a damaged physical item, please contact us at WanderingPineGames@gmail.com and we'll make it right.

🎲 Gameplay Questions

No experience needed! Our games are specifically designed to be easy to learn and start quickly. The One Night Worlds line is especially great for first-time players — everything you need is in the book and games are designed to run in a single session.
Each game lists its recommended player count on the product page. Generally, our games work well with 3–5 players including the game facilitator, but check the individual game pages for specifics.
One Night Worlds adventures are designed to run in 2–4 hours — a single evening. Full campaign games like Terminus X7Z and In Alleys Dark are designed for ongoing play with sessions typically running 2–3 hours each.
Our games use a facilitator role similar to a GM.
The Terminus X7Z and In Alleys Dark One Night Worlds are set in the same universes as their full campaign counterparts — the Latticeverse and Rankston respectively. They're great standalone experiences and also perfect introductions to the full campaign games. The Fires of Chaterra and Tales of Clockwork & Chaos are standalone settings.

🎁 Free Game & Newsletter

Sign up for our newsletter on the homepage and you'll receive a download link for What Really Happened in your welcome email.
What Really Happened is a TTRPG designed by Brian where players take on the role of characters who were actually present in the events of a favorite book — and believe the author got the story completely wrong. It's a fun, rules-light game that works with any story your group loves.
We keep our emails to the essentials — new game releases, major announcements, and occasionally behind-the-scenes content. No spam, and you can unsubscribe any time.
